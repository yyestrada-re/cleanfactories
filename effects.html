Factories are major contributors to air pollution. According to the EPA, 22% of greenhouse gases emitted by the economic sector in 2018 were from industry. Burning fossil fuels and other organic material in factories is the source of several air pollutants, primarily nitrogen oxides, which can cause respiratory irritation and asthma and create ozone, sulfur dioxide, which can lead to bronchitis and emphysema, and particulate matter, which can accumulate in and damage lung tissue and increases the likelihood of heart disease and lung cancer. The presence of these pollutants can also lead to the formation of acid rain. Acid rain has many detrimental impacts on the environment, including leaching nutrients from the soil, increasing aluminum levels in soil (which can be toxic to humans), corroding buildings, and disrupting marine ecosystems. Clearly, the effects of factory activity on the environment are damaging, but how can we fix them?

How Air Has Been Affected by COVID-19

Across the world, economic slowdowns have reduced the rate of industrial production, pausing the emission of greenhouse gases and resulting in cleaner air. Following the COVID-19 outbreak in China, NASA‚Äôs Aura satellite and ESA‚Äôs Sentinel-5 satellite have observed a significant drop in nitrogen dioxide levels over China. Nitrogen dioxide is a gas produced by industrial production, automobiles, and power plants. It is also a precursor to ozone, which can be harmful to our lungs, so it is important to limit its emission. Similar declines in nitrogen dioxide levels have been observed by NASA across South America. Air pollution is the cause of many respiratory illnesses that may make some people in polluted cities more susceptible to the virus, meaning that air pollution may increase the severity of COVID-19 outbreaks. COVID-19 has shown us how the world looks without air pollution and emphasized the importance of reducing it.

Our Solution

We propose using more scrubbers, which remove pollutants from factories before they leave the smokestacks, to reduce the levels of air pollution. We also suggest expanding the Clean Air Act, which gives factories incentives to limit their sulfur dioxide emissions below a certain level. These actions will take steps towards improving our air quality.
